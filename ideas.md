# Approach 1: Civic Industrial Stewardship

**Design Movement:** Regional industrial modernism shaped by public-infrastructure graphics, workshop pragmatism, and environmental stewardship.

**Core Principles:** The interface should communicate operational credibility before ornament. Large typographic anchors should create trust and readability at a glance. Photography should do most of the emotional work, while structure and spacing convey professionalism. Green should function as a signal of ecological action rather than as decorative branding noise.

**Color Philosophy:** The palette should begin with limestone white and weathered steel gray, then introduce kiln green and bright safety-lime as controlled accents. This approach mirrors the company’s dual identity: heavy-duty industrial capability and sustainability leadership. Warm walnut brown should appear sparingly in imagery and small surfaces so the site feels grounded in material reality rather than themed like a rustic craft store.

**Layout Paradigm:** A longitudinal, section-led landing page with strong horizontal bands, offset content blocks, and occasional full-bleed imagery. Avoid a uniformly centered composition. Instead, alternate left-weighted text panels and wide visual fields so the site feels like a guided industrial tour.

**Signature Elements:** A translucent green hero panel over a panoramic landscape image, thin technical-outline borders in bright green, and shallow arc dividers that reference the current homepage without copying Squarespace mechanics directly.

**Interaction Philosophy:** Interactions should feel precise and utility-driven. Hover states should resemble technical highlighting rather than playful movement. Buttons should respond with subtle lift, border illumination, and measured transitions that reinforce competence.

**Animation:** Use restrained reveal animations with short upward fades, slow image parallax in the hero, and gentle card motion on hover. Motion should suggest confidence and mechanical smoothness, never softness or whimsy.

**Typography System:** Use **Barlow Condensed** for high-impact headlines and navigation, paired with **Source Serif 4** for short brand statements or emphasized environmental claims, and **Source Sans 3** for body text. Headlines should be uppercase or near-uppercase with disciplined spacing; body copy should remain spacious and highly legible.

# Approach 2: Material Archive

**Design Movement:** Contemporary editorial craftsmanship inspired by museum archives, salvage catalogs, and fine furniture lookbooks.

**Core Principles:** The site should foreground provenance, process, and material richness. Sections should feel curated rather than promotional. The structure should slow the visitor down just enough to appreciate the company’s transformation of rescued trees into enduring products.

**Color Philosophy:** A quiet base of parchment, soot, tannin, and smoked oak should be punctuated by muted forest green. Instead of bright contrast, emotional warmth should come from tonal layering and wood photography. The green becomes an institutional stamp of sustainability rather than the main visual field.

**Layout Paradigm:** Editorial strips, staggered columns, inset captions, and furniture-gallery compositions with asymmetrical negative space. The page should feel like a crafted archive, not a marketing funnel.

**Signature Elements:** Captioned image frames, material swatch dividers, and understated rule lines that resemble catalog indexing. Repeated figure-and-caption structures would make services and furniture feel equally curated.

**Interaction Philosophy:** Interactions should feel calm and tactile. Hover states should reveal metadata, captions, or slight image zooms, as if the visitor is examining documented pieces in an archive.

**Animation:** Use slow dissolves, lateral slide reveals, and gentle image scaling. Section entrances should be soft and layered, echoing an editorial page turn rather than app-like transitions.

**Typography System:** Use **Cormorant Garamond** for display moments, paired with **IBM Plex Sans** for body and interface text. Typography should emphasize contrast between refined storytelling and technical service clarity.

# Approach 3: Landscape Utility

**Design Movement:** Eco-infrastructure signage blended with contemporary outdoor brand systems and workshop logistics.

**Core Principles:** The site should feel open, directional, and place-based. Content should be easy to scan from a distance, as if designed for builders, institutions, and landowners making practical decisions. The visual language should connect Texas landscape scale with urban tree reuse.

**Color Philosophy:** Sky blue, river blue-green, limestone, deep cypress, and sun-faded cedar should define the atmosphere. This palette would lean more into place than into machinery, positioning the brand as a regional environmental system rather than only a sawmill.

**Layout Paradigm:** Wide environmental sections, directional cues, anchored side labels, and expansive bands that borrow from trail systems and public signage. The composition should move laterally and diagonally instead of stacking identical centered rows.

**Signature Elements:** Oversized directional arrows, map-like contour accents, and sectional labels pinned to image edges. Curved dividers can remain, but they should be framed as terrain transitions rather than generic decorative waves.

**Interaction Philosophy:** Interactions should guide visitors clearly from mission to services to contact. Buttons and links should feel like wayfinding markers, with strong focus states and directional arrows.

**Animation:** Use sweeping wipes, horizon-like reveals, and subtle directional movement that implies travel through landscape. Motion should be clear and guiding, not ornamental.

**Typography System:** Use **Archivo Narrow** for labels and navigation, paired with **Literata** for mission statements and **Work Sans** for body content. The hierarchy should balance public-facing clarity with regional warmth.
