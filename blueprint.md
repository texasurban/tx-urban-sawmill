# Texas Urban Sawmill Website Blueprint

## Design direction
The rebuild will follow the **Civic Industrial Stewardship** philosophy. The site will preserve the recognizable character of the current Squarespace homepage: a light header, a landscape-led hero, strong green sustainability cues, service-forward storytelling, and a blend of industrial capability with handcrafted furniture credibility. The goal is not to reinvent the brand, but to recreate it in a cleaner and more maintainable Manus-hosted build.

## Structural plan
The homepage will remain a long-form, section-based experience. The opening header will keep the current logic of logo, navigation, and social links. The hero will use a panoramic Texas landscape image with a translucent green content panel and a direct primary call to action. Below that, the page will move through an impact-oriented introduction, partner credibility, services, featured furniture, climate-smart messaging, company story, newsletter prompt, and a footer with contact information and social links. This sequence mirrors the current site’s storytelling rhythm, but the implementation will be more responsive and consistent.

| Section | Purpose | Planned treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Header | Immediate brand recognition and navigation | Left-aligned logo, compact nav, social icons, sticky behavior on scroll |
| Hero | Preserve the current emotional first impression | Full-width landscape image, green glass panel, bold white heading, call-to-action |
| Impact Intro | Reproduce mission and sustainability proof points | Two-column layout with lead copy and linked achievements |
| Partners | Show institutional trust | Clean logo band with restrained motion |
| Services | Present operational breadth clearly | Responsive card grid with subtle industrial detailing |
| Furniture | Showcase heirloom craftsmanship | Large editorial image plus highlighted project cards |
| Climate Smart | Reinforce environmental differentiation | Darker contrast band with concise messaging and metric emphasis |
| About | Humanize the business | Image-plus-copy composition with family-owned positioning |
| Mailing List / Contact | Support conversion | Compact form-style callout and direct contact details |
| Footer | Keep sitewide consistency | Contact, email, phone, social, and utility navigation |

## Visual system
The visual palette will center on limestone white, weathered charcoal, kiln green, bright safety-lime accents, and warm walnut wood tones. This matches the current site’s balance of environmental optimism and industrial seriousness. Typography will use a condensed display face for headings and navigation, paired with a readable sans serif for body copy, with limited serif use only if needed for emphasized mission statements. Rounded corners will be used sparingly; the site should feel sturdy, not soft. Motion will remain restrained, using measured fades, slight lifts, and panel reveals rather than flashy animation.

## Asset plan
The rebuild will use the **original Squarespace photography and brand assets as the primary visual source**. The Squarespace-derived logo will remain the primary brand mark, and existing homepage and portfolio imagery will be reused wherever possible so the Manus-hosted version stays faithful to the current site. Newly prepared imagery will only be used as a fallback if a needed original asset is missing, too low-resolution, or otherwise unsuitable. Existing site content will be preserved closely, with small edits only where necessary for clarity or layout.

| Asset role | Planned source | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Logo | Existing site asset | Preserve brand recognition |
| Hero image | Existing Squarespace source image | Preserve the familiar first impression from the current site |
| Services image | Existing Squarespace source image | Reuse workshop or process photography from the live site |
| Furniture image | Existing Squarespace source image | Reuse the established furniture showcase imagery |
| About image | Existing Squarespace source image | Reuse site photography that connects mission to place and operation |
| Secondary logos / social | Existing brand references | Keep recognition and familiarity |

## Interaction plan
The site will remain easy to scan and practical to use. Navigation will scroll to sections with clear hover and focus states. Buttons will retain high contrast and direct language. Cards and images will have subtle motion to give the page more polish than Squarespace while staying faithful to the original tone. On mobile, the layout will stack cleanly without losing the strong hierarchy of the desktop composition.

## Implementation standard
Every edited page, component, and styling file will carry a short top-of-file reminder of the chosen design philosophy so the build stays visually disciplined. During implementation, each major decision will be checked against a simple standard: **does this reinforce or dilute Civic Industrial Stewardship?**
